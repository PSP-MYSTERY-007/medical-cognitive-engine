## 🏗️ Existing Code: Optimization & Containers

### 1. Qdrant Containers (Human vs. Specialty)

* **Opinion:** **Partially okay, but don't over-segment.** * **The "Why":** Splitting "Guidelines" (CPG) from "Textbooks" is excellent because they serve different purposes (Action vs. Theory). However, creating a separate container/collection for *every* specialty (Cardiology, Neuro, etc.) can lead to "siloing." A doctor might ask about a Diabetic patient (Endo) with Heart Failure (Cardio).
* **Better Approach:** Keep **two main collections** (CPG and Textbook) but use **metadata filtering**. When you ingest, add a tag `{"specialty": "cardiology"}`. This allows you to search *only* Cardiology or *everything* depending on the doctor's query.

### 2. Incremental Ingestion Script

* **Opinion:** **Essential (Must have).**
* **The "Why":** Re-ingesting hundreds of PDFs every time is a waste of time and compute.
* **Implementation Tip:** Use a **hashing system**. Before processing a PDF, calculate its MD5 hash. If that hash exists in your "Processed Log," skip it. This ensures you only ingest truly new or updated documents.

## 🚀 New Features: Evaluation

### 1. Multi-Agent Verification (Consensus)

* **Opinion:** **High Value.** * **The "Why":** This is the "Consultant Node" logic. Having one agent retrieve and a second agent specifically look for **contradictions** between the CPG and Textbook is exactly how doctors think.
* **Action:** Keep the "Logic Guard" to ensure the AI doesn't hallucinate if retrieval scores are low.

### 2. Knowledge Graph (KG) Integration

* **Opinion:** **Extremely High Value (The "Holy Grail" of Medical RAG).**
* **The "Why":** Vector search (Qdrant) finds "similar words." A Knowledge Graph understands "relationships" (e.g., *Drug A* ➡️ *inhibits* ➡️ *Enzyme B* ➡️ *causes* ➡️ *Side Effect C*).
* **Addition:** Instruct the KG to specifically flag **Missing Parameters**. If the KG knows "Drug X requires eGFR," and the prompt doesn't have it, the AI can stop and ask for it.

### 3. "Last Updated" Tracker

* **Opinion:** **Critical for Safety.**
* **The "Why":** A 2024 CPG should always override a 2018 Textbook.
* **Action:** Add a `publication_date` field to your Qdrant payload. The LLM should be prompted to say: *"Based on the 2024 Guidelines (more recent than the 2020 Textbook)..."*

### 4. Human-in-the-Loop (Feedback)

* **Opinion:** **Necessary for Legal/Audit.**
* **The "Why":** Doctors will only trust the system if they can "correct" it.
* **Action:** Store flagged responses in a "Redline" database. Use this data later to fine-tune your prompts or re-ranker.

### 5. Pharmacopoeia Integration

* **Opinion:** **Great, but keep it structured.**
* **The "Why":** LLMs are bad at reading messy tables from PDFs.
* **Action:** Do not just "ingest" the Pharmacopoeia as text. Convert it into a **CSV/JSON database**. When a drug is mentioned, the AI should do a "Direct Lookup" in the table rather than a "Semantic Search" in a PDF. This guarantees 100% dosage accuracy.
